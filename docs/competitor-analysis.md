# Competitor Analysis

| Product | Pricing | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---------|---------|-----------|------------|
| **Jeeva** | ₹499-2999/mo | Popular in India, biometric support | Dated UI, complex setup |
| **GymMaster** | USD-based | Feature-rich | Too expensive for small gyms, not India-focused |
| **Zoho Gym** | ₹Free-999/mo | Zoho ecosystem | Generic, not gym-specific enough |

| **MyPTHub** | USD-based | PT-focused | Personal trainers only, not gym management |

## Gaps We're Exploiting

### 1. Setup Complexity
Most competitors require 30-60 min setup, training calls, and demo sessions. GymFlow Track targets **10-minute self-serve onboarding**.

### 2. WhatsApp-First
Indian gym owners communicate with members via WhatsApp. No competitor has native WhatsApp integration as a primary workflow (most use SMS or email).

### 3. Modern UI/UX
Most Indian gym software looks like it was built in 2010. GymFlow Track uses modern design patterns that non-technical users actually enjoy using.

### 4. Mobile-First Dashboard
Gym owners check things on their phone. Most competitors have desktop-first dashboards that break on mobile.

### 5. Pricing
Target: ₹299-799/month — affordable for any gym making ₹50K+/month revenue.

## Our Anti-Positioning

We do NOT compete on:
- Feature count (less is more)
- Enterprise deals
- Biometric hardware
- Workout tracking

We WIN on:
- Speed of setup
- Daily usability
- WhatsApp integration
- Modern design
- Mobile experience
